Guidelines for expressing proteins in Minimal Media

Here are two alternative protocols that are likely to work well:
[I]
1. Transform bacteria in LB plate.
2. Pick a colony (~1 mm in diameter) from the plate and streak a MM plate.
3. Pick a colony (at least ~0.5 mm in diameter) from MM plate and inoculate liquid MM culture.

[II]
1. Streak a MM plate with a glycerol stock of already transformed bacteria.
2. Pick a colony (at least ~0.5 mm in diameter) from MM plate and inoculate liquid MM culture. 

Some practical tips:
It takes about 36-48 h for discrete colonies to appear on MM plates when grown at 37 deg C.  Inoculate 100 ml MM cultures around at 8 or 9 PM and let them grow for 12 hours.  The OD600 should be ~0.8.  Inoculate 1 liter cultures.  The doubling time should be ~1 hour.  The key to getting high levels of expression in MM is to (a) keep bugs growing constantly (i.e. do not transfer plates/liquid cultures to the refrigerator at any stage) and (b) ensure that the OD600 is ideally below 0.8 and definitely below 1.0.  The latter is crucial because after the exponential growth phase, bacteria enter a state of stasis and are "too old" to make much protein.  Growth kinetics (i.e. doubling times) are an excellent indicator of the health and happiness level of bacteria (like your pulse rate and other vital signs), so be sure to monitor this closely.
