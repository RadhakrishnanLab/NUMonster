PD 10 Sephadex Desalting Column for Refolding Proteins

 

May be used as a size-exclusion column for crude separation of a protein from 8M Urea in order to spontaneously refold the protein.  The maximum volume of the column is 5 mL, and this should be considered one “column volume.”  The volume of protein added should be around 2.5 mL

 

Warning: The protein may crash out of solution and get trapped in the column, or simply bind the column and not elute.  Only try this as a refolding method as a last resort!!

 

A.     Prepare the column

1.      Remove the column from the package and suspend over a waste container with a stand and clamp

2.      Cut off the sealed tip and allow the ethanol storage buffer to drain from the column

3.      Wash with 5 column volumes of UV/UF water (~25 mL)

4.      Wash with 5 column volumes of appropriate buffer

 

B.     Load protein

1.      When the final buffer wash has entered the column, add 2.5 mL of protein to the column

2.      When all of the protein has entered the resin, immediately add 3.5 mL of buffer – this will ensure all of the protein will flow through the resin. 

 

C.     Collect gravity flow-through

1.      Set up 12-15 Eppendorf tubes to collect the protein

2.      The protein will start dripping from the column tip in drops of approximately 50mL.  Collect ~ 10 drops per Eppendorf tube for a total of approx 0.5 mL per fraction.  The protein should flow through in the first 6 mL, so collect at least twelve 0.5mL fractions.

3.      Run the 12 fractions on a SDS-PAGE gel to identify which fractions contain the protein.
